The title characters of this clever first novel land in 1899 New York, where their magic often makes more sense than the ways of the humans with whom they now live. TO SAVE EVERYTHING, CLICK HERE: The Folly of Technological Solutionism, by Evgeny Morozov (PublicAffairs, $28.99.) Digital-age transparency may threaten the spirit of democracy, Morozov warns. CONSTANCE, by Patrick McGrath (Bloomsbury, $25.) In McGrath’s dark and ecstatic novel, a psychologically fragile young woman discovers a family secret that sends her teetering into moral chaos. ALL THE GREAT PRIZES: The Life of John Hay, From Lincoln to Roosevelt, by John Taliaferro (Simon & Schuster, $35.) From the birth of the Republican Party to the Spanish-American War, Hay was a seminal figure in 19th-century government.
