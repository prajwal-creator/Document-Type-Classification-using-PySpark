The continuing promising outlook for business in the heavy goods industries in conjunction with the large money supply make for optimism in the short-range economic outlook, but other economic forces point to an early exhaustion of the inflationary boom, possibly -- preferably, too -- in 1948, Dr. Marcus Nadler of New York University told the finance conference of the American Management Association in the Biltmore Hotel yesterday.
