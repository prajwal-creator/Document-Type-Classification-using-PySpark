In the low, rambling wooden Assembly Hall on Ward's Island 1,200 of the 6,000 patients of the Manhattan State Insane Asylum were watching last night the big screen where the regular weekly program of movies was flashing. Watching, too, from the second row was Dr. Marcus B. Heyman, Superintendent of the asylum, and his family.
