QUITE late the other night I boarded a taxi in midtown Manhattan to go to Brooklyn Heights. It was a big, old-fashioned taxicab -- the kind with jump seats -- and the front seat was separated from the back by a piece of warpy plexiglass, apparently designed to discourage holdup men armed with water pistols.
