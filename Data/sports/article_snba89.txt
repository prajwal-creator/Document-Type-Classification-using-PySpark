After two days of discussions, the White Sox chairman, Jerry Reinsdorf, said in Chicago, ''Himes was not fired and he did not resign.'' But Reinsdorf added: ''Larry Himes took us from point A to point B. It's our opinion Larry Himes is not the best person to get us to point C: a world's championship.'' Himes, 49 years old, joined the White Sox in October 1986 as vice president and general manager. He was named senior vice president the next year. I am proud of how the young players we have acquired in trades have stepped in and produced.'' He did not say why he was departing.
