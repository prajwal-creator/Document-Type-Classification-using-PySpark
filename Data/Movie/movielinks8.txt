AS an image added to a montage of good works in the entertainment arts, Richard Schickel's "Movies" is proof of dedication and versatility. He is the author of "The World of Carnegie Hall," printed just before that fortress of the fugue was saved from demolition, and "The Stars," a text-and-picture paean to the screen's once crowded, shining galaxy.
