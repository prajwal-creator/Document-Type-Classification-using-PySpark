Defender Ondrej Celustka scored in the 76th minute, pouncing on the rebound of a shot from Halil Altintop that hit the crossbar at the San Siro in Milan. Trabzonspor gained a spot in the group stage only after another Turkish club, Fenerbahce, was removed by the Turkish Football Federation because it was under suspicion in a criminal probe into match-fixing. The loss deepens an early season crisis for Inter, which hast lost its first three games: against A.C. Milan in the Italian Super Cup last month, at Palermo in its Serie A opener and now its Champions League opener.  Photo        Trabzonspor shocked Inter Milan with a 1-0 victory on Wednesday.   Credit Olivier Morin/Agence France-Presse — Getty Images    Coach Gian Piero Gasperini’s job could be at risk if Inter does not beat Roma on Saturday. In the other Group B game, visiting CSKA of Moscow rallied to draw Lille, 2-2, behind two goals by Seydou Doumbia. Aleksandar Kolarov equalized with a superb free kick to salvage a 1-1 tie for host Manchester City against Napoli. Continue reading the main story         Advertisement   Continue reading the main story       City was playing in its first match in the Champions League, Europe’s elite competition, since 1968. It was Napoli’s first game at that level since 1990, when Diego Maradona was part of its squad. In the other Group A game, Bayern Munich won at Villareal, 2-0. Angel Di Maria scored in the 53rd minute as Real Madrid started its Champions League campaign with a 1-0 win at Dinamo Zagreb. Cristiano Ronaldo, Karim Benzema and Marcelo combined to set up Di Maria. Marcelo was sent off in the 73rd minute when he earned a second yellow card. The other Group D game, Ajax vs. Lyon in Amsterdam, ended goalless.
