Is Trying to Raise $1 Billion by Turn of Century    AP    Continue reading the main story  Share This Page   Continue reading the main story                     , Page 001072 The New York Times Archives    The president of Boston University has begun a fund-raising drive to raise $1 billion by the turn of the century to triple the school's endowment and build space for a variety of projects. ''Either we stand still or we go ahead,'' said the school's president, Dr. John R. Silber. ''We're at the takeoff point to become one of the 10 best universities in the nation.'' The projects include renovating existing buildings and building new ones for a school of management, a center for performing arts, a new athletics center and more student dormitories. The university, which has nearly 20,000 students, has an endowment of $165 million, which Dr. Silber wants increased to $665 million.   Advertisement   Continue reading the main story   The university is setting up a Turn of the Century Foundation under the direction of Arthur G. B. Metcalf, the electronics industrialist, to seek long-term bequests. $75 Million Annual Goal Mr. Metcalf, who is chairman of the university's trustees, has given the university $7 million in the last four years. The project will require raising $75 million yearly for the next 12 years. Dr. Silber said Boston University had been able to raise $50 million each year for the last four years.
