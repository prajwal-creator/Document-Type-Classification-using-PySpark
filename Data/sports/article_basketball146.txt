Loses First Game Without Center   By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS MARCH 2, 2011     Continue reading the main story  Share This Page   Continue reading the main story           Phillip McDonald scored 26 points to lead New Mexico to an 82-64 upset of No. 3 Brigham Young on Wednesday, just two days after the Cougars dismissed their leading rebounder, center Brandon Davies, for a violation of the university’s honor code. Jimmer Fredette had 33 points but it came on 10-of-26 shooting, including 1 of 9 from 3-point range, in a game in which B.Y.U. (27-3, 13-2 Mountain West) tried to find the right combination on the court without Davies. The Cougars started the 6-foot-10 junior James Anderson in Davies’s place, but Coach Dave Rose quickly went to Plan B and inserted Kyle Collinsworth. Not much seemed to work as B.Y.U.’s inside game disappeared. The Cougars were outrebounded by 25-14 in the first half as the Lobos (19-11, 7-8) opened a 42-26 lead. XAVIER 68, CHARLOTTE 48 The senior guard Dante Jackson scored 18 points as No. 23 Xavier  (23-6, 14-1) secured at least a share of its fifth straight Atlantic 10 regular-season title. IN OTHER GAMES Jonathan Mitchell scored 18 points to help visiting Rutgers (14-15, 5-12 Big East) hold off DePaul, 68-64. ... Aaric Murray had 20 points and 13 rebounds as La Salle dominated Fordham, 86-61, handing the Rams (6-21, 0-15 Atlantic 10) their 17th straight loss. ... Visiting Lafayette never trailed in a 77-70 victory over Holy Cross in the Patriot League quarterfinals. Lehigh, Bucknell and American also advanced to the semifinals. Women BAYLOR 84, MISSOURI 52 Brittney Griner had 22 points and 5 blocked shots as third-ranked Baylor clinched the Big 12 title at home. The Lady Bears’ win clinched a share of the title, and they won it outright a few minutes later when No. 5  Texas A&M lost at Kansas State, 71-67.
