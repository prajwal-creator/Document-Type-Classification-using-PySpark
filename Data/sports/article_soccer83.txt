Timed tickets are available hourly beginning at 10:30 a.m., with the last ticket sold each day at 4:30 p.m.; the museum is open from 10 a.m. to 5:45 p.m. Central Park West and 79th Street, (212) 769-5100, amnh.org ; $25, $14.50 for children 2 to 12 years old, or $19 for 60+ and students, with a $2 surcharge when ordering tickets online. Asia Society and Museum: ‘Ai Weiwei — New York Photographs 1983-1993’ (through Sunday) This is the last weekend to view a display of more than 200 photographs taken by Ai Weiwei, the Chinese artist and activist who was recently released from detention in Beijing. Hours are 11 a.m. to 6 p.m. on all three days. 725 Park Avenue, at 70th Street, (212) 288-6400, asiasociety.org ; $10; $7 for 65+; $5 for students and free for members and those under 16. Discovery Times Square: ‘Pompeii the Exhibit’ (Friday through Thursday) This exhibition, subtitled “Life and Death in the Shadow of Vesuvius,” features skeletons, body casts and other artifacts from Pompeii. Through Sept. 5. Friday, Saturday and Thursday, 9 a.m. to 9 p.m., and Sunday through Wednesday, 9 a.m. to 8 p.m. Discovery Times Square, 226 West 44th Street, Manhattan, (212) 987-9692, discoveryts.com ; $26; $22.50 for 65+; $19.50 for children ages 4 to 12 years. Museum of the Moving Image: ‘The Films of Frank Sinatra’ (Friday through Sunday) For the next four weekends a series devoted to Frank Sinatra will feature films that are little known as well as those with which he is strongly identified. “Suddenly!,” a 1954 film in which Sinatra played one of a group of assassins plotting to kill the president, will be shown on Saturday at 7 p.m. Other films this weekend are “On the Town” (1949), Friday at 7 p.m. and Sunday at 4 p.m., and “From Here to Eternity” (1953), Saturday at 4 p.m. and Sunday at 7 p.m. 35th Avenue at 37th Street, Astoria, Queens, (718) 777-6800, movingimage.us ; $12, $9 for 65+ and students, $6 for children 3 and younger and members. Continue reading the main story         Advertisement   Continue reading the main story       Events Alphabet City Dolly Film Festival (Friday and Saturday) Films will be served with a twist at more than a dozen nightspots in the East Village during this festival of more than 20 shorts, documentaries and feature films. Among the features this weekend is “Leave No Soldier,” a 2008 documentary by Donna Bassin, a psychologist and filmmaker, about veterans and the challenges they face in returning to their former lives. Tickets, at $20 for both days, are available at Mama’s Bar, 34 Avenue B, at Third Street, and Bar on A, 170 Avenue A, at 11th Street. Locations and screenings, abcdfilmfestival.com ; for other information, (646) 707-1422. Astoria Park Shore Fest (Sunday) For the second of three Sundays, the boulevard that borders Astoria Park in Queens and the East River will be closed to traffic and open to parkgoers from noon to 7 p.m. for a free outdoor celebration with games, performances, crafts and food. Formerly called the Astoria Water Walk, the event is sponsored by the Astoria Park Alliance, with Green Shores NYC, the city’s departments of parks and transportation, and other organizations. The third and last day of the event will be Aug. 21. Shore Boulevard, between Astoria Park South and Ditmars Boulevard, astoriaparkshorefest.yolasite.com . Coney Island Entertainment (Friday through Monday) Music and film buffs, as well as sideshow enthusiasts, will have their pick of events along the Coney Island boardwalk. Demonstrations of sword swallowing and snake charming are part of the Coney Island Circus Sideshow, playing daily from 1 to 8 p.m. at Surf Avenue and West 12th Street, www.coneyisland.com ; $10, or $5 for children under 12. There will be a free festival of rock music on Saturday from 2 to 8 p.m. in front of Deno’s Wonder Wheel, at West 12th Street; (718) 372-2592. Dancing, along with volleyball and soccer, will be part of the Coney Island Reggae Beach Party, on Sunday from 2 to 9 p.m. on West 19th Street. The party — sponsored by 89.9, WKCR-FM, and the nonprofit group Sound Liberation Front — is free; brooklyn.com/events.html . A tour of historical spots on Coney Island, starting at 12:15 p.m. on Sunday, will leave from the Coney Island History Project on West 12th Street; the tour is $20, and reservations can be made at www.coneyislandhistory.org . And for those film buffs? “Moonstruck” (1987), starring Cher and Nicolas Cage, will be shown on Monday at 8:40 p.m. at Surf Avenue and West 10th Street as part of the free Flicks on the Beach series; brooklyn.com/events.html . Dance of the Giglio Feast (Friday through Sunday) This annual celebration of music and strength — with the hoisting of a platform containing a tower and a band of musicians — will be celebrated on the street outside of Our Lady of Mt. Carmel Shrine Church. Festivities this weekend include entertainment on Friday from 8 to 11 p.m.; a procession on Saturday beginning at noon; and the lifting and “Dance of the Giglio di Sant’Antonio,” with music, beginning on Sunday at 1:30 p.m. Sponsored by the Giglio Society of East Harlem of Our Lady of Mount Carmel Shrine Church. Pleasant Avenue, between 114th and 116th Streets, East Harlem, eastharlemgiglio.com . A walking tour of the neighborhood, with sampling of local delicacies, will be led by Noshwalks on Sunday at 1 p.m., meeting in front of the Malcolm Shabazz Harlem Market, 52 West 116th Street; $49; $44 for subscribers; $16 for children; (212) 222-2243, noshwalks.com . Dominican Day Parade (Sunday) The parade begins at 1 p.m. at Avenue of the Americas and 36th Street and travels north to 56th Street. Dream Up Festival (Sunday through Thursday) A three-week festival of experimental theater and dance will fill the performance spaces of the theater through Sept. 4. Theater for the New City, 155 First Avenue, at Ninth Street, East Village; (212) 254-1109, (212) 868-4444, theaterforthenewcity.net ; $12 to $15. Free Events in Bryant Park (Friday, Saturday, Monday and Tuesday) Free events continue, weather permitting. For those who already feel like they’re juggling things every day, instruction in the physical art of juggling will be offered from 1 to 3 p.m. on Friday. Modern dance classes given by Limón Dance Company continue on Saturday at 11 a.m. “High Sierra,” the 1941 film with Humphrey Bogart and Ida Lupino, will be shown on Monday at dusk as part of a film festival co-sponsored by HBO. And on Tuesday at 7 p.m., there will be a poetry reading also sponsored by Letras Latinas, the literary program of the Institute for Latino Studies at Notre Dame. (212) 768-4242, bryantpark.org . Hong Kong Dragon Boat Festival (Saturday and Sunday) Dragon boat teams from across the country and Canada will take part in this annual competition on Meadow Lake in Flushing Meadows-Corona Park, Queens. The tradition commemorates the life and death of the ancient Chinese poet and reformer Qu Yuan, who drowned himself to protest government policies; attempts to rescue him by boat failed, and the tradition of boat racing began. In addition to the races, which run from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. both days, there will be food and demonstrations of Chinese culture, including dance, crafts and martial arts displays. hkdbf-ny.org ; free. It will go on from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. and will include music, food, children’s activities, a raffle and a best-in-show contest, with the winner chosen by visitors. Admission is free; raffle tickets are $5, with a portion of the proceeds to benefit City Harvest. volkswagentrafficjam.com ; ferry information at govisland.com . Outdoor ‘The Taming of the Shrew’ (Friday through Sunday, and Thursday) A production of Shakespeare’s “Taming of the Shrew” is closing the summer season of the Hudson Warehouse at the Soldiers’ and Sailors’ Monument in Riverside Park. Directed by Jesse Michael Mothershed, who played Falstaff in the company’s production of “The Merry Wives of Windsor” in June, the show will run through Aug. 28. At 6:30 p.m., Riverside Drive and West 89th Street, hudsonwarehouse.net ; free, with donations accepted. No show if it rains. Summer Seaport Festival (Saturday) 11 a.m. to 6 p.m., on Water Street, between Fulton and Broad Streets, Lower Manhattan. Village Visiting Neighbors Street Fair (Saturday) 11 a.m. to 6 p.m., on University Place, between Waverly Place and 14th Street, Greenwich Village. Spoken Word ‘Theater of War’ (Monday) At 7 p.m. a reading of “Ajax,” Sophocles’ tale of the ancient Greek warrior, is part of an evening of theater and dialogue at the Malcolm X & Dr. Betty Shabazz Memorial and Educational Center, 3940 Broadway, near 165th Street, Washington Heights. Reg E. Cathey of the HBO shows “The Wire” and “Oz” will join the Classical Theater of Harlem in the reading, which is free and sponsored by Theater of War Productions. It will be followed by a discussion with a panel including veterans, the mother of a soldier killed in Iraq and representatives from military and health organizations, with the audience also encouraged to take part. The event will be repeated on Aug. 22, with a different cast and program. Reservations are necessary and can be made by calling (347) 688-6304 or by e-mail at cth2011@gmail.com . Event information is available at classicaltheaterofharlem.org , and more details about the community projects of Theater of War Productions can be found at theater-of-war.com . Walking Tours ‘Foods of the Lower East Side’ (Friday and Saturday) The immigrant history of the Lower East Side will be observed and tasted on a tour of the area that includes stops for food sampling; sponsored by the Lower East Side Tenement Museum, the tour meets at 1 p.m. at the Visitors Center, 108 Orchard Street, and costs $45; $40 for children, students and 65+; or $22 for members. The museum is at 91 Orchard Street. (866) 606-7232, tenement.org . ‘Ground Zero and Its Neighbors — The First 10 Years’ (Friday) The construction and controversies near the site of the terrorist attacks of 9/11 will be covered on this tour, led by Joyce Gold History Tours of New York; it meets at 1 p.m. near St. Paul’s Chapel, Broadway and Vesey Street. (212) 242-5762, joycegoldhistorytours.com ; $15, or $12 for 62+.   Advertisement   Continue reading the main story   Grand Central Terminal Walking Tour (Wednesday) The historic terminal, which dates to 1913, is the subject of weekly tours on Wednesdays at 12:30 p.m. led by the Municipal Art Society, meeting at the information booth in the main concourse. Photo IDs are required: (212) 935-2075, mas.org/tours ; $10. Jazz Tour of Bedford-Stuyvesant (Friday) A walk through this historic brownstone neighborhood in Brooklyn will be followed by a jazz concert, with refreshments. The tour, sponsored by Metro Tour Service, meets at 6 p.m. in Brooklyn at 107 Macon Street, near Nostrand Avenue. (718) 789-0430; $40. TriBeCa Walk: A Salute to Robert De Niro (Saturday) A walk to celebrate the 68th birthday of the actor — born Aug. 17, 1943 — will meet at 3 p.m. on the northwest corner of Franklin Street and West Broadway. It is sponsored by Adventure on a Shoestring. (212) 265-2663; $10. Tubby Hook Walking Tour (Sunday) This Upper Manhattan area, between Fort Tryon Park and Inwood Hill Park, was a strategic site during the Revolutionary War. James Renner, an urban historian, will lead a tour of the neighborhood, meeting at noon on the northwest corner of Dyckman Street and Broadway. (917) 533-3252; $15, or $12 for 65+. Union Square: Crossroads of New York (Saturday) A tour focusing on the area’s political history, meeting at 2 p.m. by the statue of Lincoln in Union Square Park, near the 16th Street transverse. Sponsored by the Union Square Partnership, (212) 517-1826, unionsquarenyc.org ; free.
