Walliser was timed in 1 minute 23.46 seconds on fresh snow in Val d'Isere, France. Catherine Quittet of France was second in 1:23.61 with Walliser's teammate, Vreni Schneider, third in 1:23.87. (AP) Pramotton Wins Again: Richard Pramotton won his second consecutive World Cup giant slalom race, leading the Italian team to a 1-2-3 sweep in Val Badia, Italy. Ingemar Stenmark, the Swedish veteran, finished fourth, skiing the fastest second heat and improving 12 places from the first run, on the same course on which he had scored his 80th World Cup victory last year. The 22-year-old Pramotton achieved his third World Cup triumph with a time of 2:45.26, edging Alberto Tomba and Oswald Toetsch.(AP) Tennis Becker Wins in Germany: Top-seeded Boris Becker of West Germany fought off a stubborn challenge from Jonas B. Svensson of Sweden in Stuttgart, West Germany, to retain the Waterford Crystal Young Masters tennis title for the third time. The 19-year-old, two-time Wimbledon winner needed 2 hours 36 minutes to beat Svensson, 7-6, 7-6, 6-3. Becker was rewarded with a check for $30,000, while Svensson earned $20,000.(AP) Track Lynch Triumphs: Shola Lynch of Hunter College High School won the 1,000-meter race and the 600 meters at the Metropolitan Athletics Congress All-Comers Track and Field Meet at Manhattan College yesterday. Lynch, a senior, was timed in 2 minutes 53.2 seconds in the 1,000 and then came back to win the 600 in 1:35.8. Samantha Jackson of Shabazz High School in Newark, N.J., won the girls' 300 meters in 41.6 seconds and Arthur Vaughn of Uniondale High School won the boys' 300 in 37.2. Sam Rice of Cardinal Hayes High School captured the 55-meter dash in 6.4 seconds. Soccer Duke 1, Akron 0: Duke won the National Collegiate Athletic Association soccer championship in Tacoma, Wash., Saturday night as Tom Stone scored the only goal, a 7-yard rising shot that hit the bottom of the crossbar and bounced into the net 1 minute 34 seconds into the second half. Mark Dodd, starting his 15th match in goal for Duke (18-5-1) after sharing the job in the Blue Devils' first five matches, had to make only three saves in notching his ninth shutout of the season. David Zupko had four saves for Akron (17-4-3)(AP) N.H.L. Sabres 4, Whalers 3: Lindy Ruff scored what proved to be the winning goal late in the second period last night as the struggling Buffalo Sabres snapped a seven-game streak without a victory at home. (AP) Horse Racing Temperate Sil by a Neck: Temperate Sil, under the Hall of Fame team of Bill Shoemaker and trainer Charles Whittingham, yesterday won by a neck the $1 million Hollywood Futurity for 2-year-olds at Hollywood Park as Brave Raj, the favorite, finished a badly beaten fifth. Shoemaker, who sets a riding record with every victory, brought Temperate Sil from eighth place early in the mile race to outgain Aly Sheba at the finish with Masterful Advocate a neck farther back in third. The winner raced the mile in 1:36 1/5, and paid $13, $7.60 and $5 for $2 across the board. Silent Turn Equals Mark: Silent Turn closed fast to overtake favored Grecian Flight in the final strides and post a neck victory in the $124,000 Tempted Stakes for 2-year-old fillies at Aqueduct. With Chris Antley aboard, Silent Turn went the mile and a sixteenth on the fast track in 1:46 1/5. Her clocking equaled the stakes record established by Willowy Mood in 1984. College Basketball Lady Longhorns Lose: Tennessee sank seven of eight free throws in the final minute yesterday to defeat Texas, 85-78, and end the defending women's national champions' winning streak at 40 games. Bridgette Gordon scored 26 points for Tennessee, which is now 5-0 for the season. Clarissa Davis scored 32 points for the Lady Longhorns, who won all 34 of their games last season and the first six games this season.(AP)    We are continually improving the quality of our text archives.
