See next articles       See previous articles           Site Navigation    Site Mobile Navigation       Advertisement          Supported by     N.Y. / Region   Robbing Students Of the Joy of Learning   FEB. 9, 2003     Continue reading the main story  Share This Page   Continue reading the main story           To the Editor: Educators' response to the ''senior slide'' (Jan. 19) is disheartening. Research shows that emphasis on motivators like grades, honors, money and test scores decreases motivation for problem solving and creativity, mastery learning and even participation in athletics. At one time, high school senior elective classes were themselves the reward for learning. Small classes where a special teacher shared a passion for a particular discipline were highly rewarding experiences in and of themselves. Our current emphasis on trophy colleges and the high importance of future income and life luxuries have robbed many of our students of these life-shaping experiences and of their joy of learning. J.B. Brandi Pottersville The writer is a professor of psychology of human learning and motivation, New Jersey City University.
