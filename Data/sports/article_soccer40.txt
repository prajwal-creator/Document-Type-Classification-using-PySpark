Here’s the latest. Image Credit Stephen Crowley/The New York Times 1. The White House announced that 81 major companies had committed to major reductions in greenhouse gas emissions , including Levi Strauss & Co., McDonald’s, IBM, Nike and Procter & Gamble. But many of the commitments were not new, just packaged together to showcase American resolve ahead of international climate talks in Paris. _______ Image Credit Kim Kyung-Hoon/Reuters 2. China released data showing its economic slowdown worsening , as its growth drivers — manufacturing and housing construction — become drags. The government’s urgent efforts to shore up stocks and get the economy back on track are running against the free-market ground rules of global investment. And the environmental toll from years of rapid growth includes warnings of possibly irreversible damage to coastal wetlands . _______ Image Credit Eric Gay/Associated Press 3. Texas halted state Medicaid funds for Planned Parenthood , citing videos made by an anti-abortion group that have been seized on by many Republicans. “The gruesome harvesting of baby body parts by Planned Parenthood will not be allowed in Texas,” Gov. Greg Abbott, a Republican, said in a statement. Planned Parenthood maintains that the videos were edited to be misleading. _______ Image Credit Qiu Pan/European Pressphoto Agency 4. News for dog lovers: A research project that included DNA tests for thousands of street dogs pointed to Central Asia as the area where “all the dogs alive today” come from. “The great thing about working with dogs is that if you show up with food you don’t usually have trouble recruiting subjects,” a lead scientist said. _______ Image Credit Abir Sultan/European Pressphoto Agency 5. Israelis have been arming themselves and training, above, in response to a wave of lethal attacks by Palestinians, many wielding knives. But the prime minister, Benjamin Netanyahu, warned, “No one should take the law into their own hands,” after an Eritrean asylum seeker was mistaken for an attacker on Sunday. He died after being shot by an Israeli soldier and then beaten by a crowd. _______ Image Credit Sergey Ponomarev for The New York Times 6. The flow of migrants across Europe has been snagging on border closures and other blocks , sometimes leaving hundreds of people outdoors overnight in rain and dropping temperatures. “We are worried they will close the border, but we are also worried about winter,” a Syrian refugee said. “We must get where we are going before the snows fall.” _______ Image Credit Scott Eells/Bloomberg News ..Published 05/11/2012 7. A mistrial was declared in the case against three lawyers who presided over the collapse of the firm Dewey & LeBoeuf , as jurors said they were “hopelessly deadlocked” after 21 days of deliberation. The Manhattan district attorney, Cyrus Vance, could retry the men, who are accused of manipulating financial records to keep bank and investor funding during the financial crisis. _______ Image Credit Nam Y. Huh/Associated Press 8. The Toronto Blue Jays beat the Kansas City Royals, 11-8, to take Game 3 of the American League Championship Series. The Royals maintain the series lead, 2-1. The New York Mets, practicing above, are 2-0 in the National League championships going into Game 3 against the Cubs in Chicago on Tuesday (8:07 p.m. Eastern, TBS). _______ Image Credit Nicholas Kamm/Agence France-Presse — Getty Images 9. Justin Trudeau, above, the son of Canada’s most glamorous leader, Pierre Trudeau, has unseated the Conservative prime minister, Stephen Harper, according to projections Monday night by Canadian broadcast networks. _______ Image Credit Ahmad Al-Rubaye/Agence France-Presse — Getty Images 10. Supported by increased American air power and Iranian Shiite militias, Iraqi forces were taking on Islamic State militants on multiple fronts. “We are doing what you always try to do to the enemy and that is force him to fight in more than one direction at the same time,” said Lt. Gen. Sean B. MacFarland, the new U.S. commander for operations in Iraq and Syria. _______ Image 11. “Star Wars”: The sales begin.  The campaign for Disney’s juggernaut “Star Wars: The Force Awakens” ramps up into high gear, with ticket sales beginning after a full trailer played during halftime of “Monday Night Football.” _______ Video A stretcher team trying to get an soccer player off the field during a match in Greece over the weekend could have used a little help themselves.
