No features of the American landscape are more serenely beautiful than the symmetrical cones of the Cascade Mountains paralleling the Pacific coast from Canada to the northern part of California. Those at the north end of the chain, like Mount Baker and Mount Rainier, are always gleaming white. The southernmost, Mount Lassen, is only so in winter.
